Prep work makes or breaks the job
The single most common complaint in 1-star reviews: peeling, bubbling, or uneven results due to skipped surface prep. The best reviews specifically call out thorough sanding, cleaning, and priming.
Sanding & priming mentioned in 71% of 5-star reviews Skipped prep = #1 complaint in 1-star reviews
"They spent almost as much time on prep as painting — scraped, sanded, primed everything. Two years later it still looks perfect." — Inner Richmond, 5 stars
3
Ask what paint brand and how many coats
Not all quotes are equal. A low bid often means cheaper paint or only one coat. Ask upfront: brand, sheen, and number of coats. Reviewers who specified Sherwin-Williams or Benjamin Moore had significantly better outcomes.
SW / BM mentioned → 4.7★ avg Generic / unspecified → 4.2★ avg 2-coat minimum recommended
4
Communicate about colors before they start — not during
Color disputes are the most common source of mid-job friction. Get samples on the actual wall in daylight. Reviewers who did color consultations upfront reported far smoother projects.
"Color looked different on the wall" — in 29% of 2-3 star reviews Test patches in multiple lights recommended
"We did sample patches in natural and artificial light before committing. Saved us from a color we would have hated — totally worth the extra day." — Noe Valley, 5 stars
5
Protect your stuff and verify cleanup expectations
Paint overspray, drips on floors, and left-behind supplies are surprisingly common gripes. The best crews protect everything proactively and clean up daily. Ask what their process is before hiring.
Overspray / floor drips in 22% of negative reviews Daily cleanup = strong predictor of overall satisfaction
"They covered every inch of flooring and wiped down surfaces at the end of each day. I was shocked — it was cleaner than when they started." — Castro, 5 stars
6
Get the timeline and touch-up policy in writing
Job delays and disappeared contractors are consistent themes in 1-star reviews. Equally important: a touch-up warranty (30–90 days is standard). Top-rated painters are upfront about both.
Delays / ghosting = #2 complaint Touch-up policy offered → 4.8★ avg 30–90 day touch-up window is standard
