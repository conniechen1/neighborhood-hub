How painters compare to other service jobs

Painters are one of the most trustworthy service categories
With an 86.3% 5-star rate and only 3.7% warning mentions, painters rank among the safest hires you can make. For comparison, auto repair has 18.5% 1-stars and HVAC has 9.9% warnings. The main risk isn't fraud ‚Äî it's scope creep and poor communication.

Communication is mentioned in 42.6% of all painter reviews
No other signal predicts the outcome of a paint job more reliably. Reviews that mention communication are overwhelmingly 5-stars. Reviews that don't mention communication ‚Äî and still end up 1-star ‚Äî almost always describe a contractor who went quiet mid-project.

19% of reviews mention the quote process ‚Äî 2nd highest of any job type
Only roofers (25%) are more quote-conscious. This tells you the quote conversation matters a lot to people ‚Äî but the actual dollar amounts almost never appear in reviews (only 4.5%). People care about the process, not just the number.
Green flags & red flags
What separates the 5-star jobs from the 1-stars
‚úì Green flags ‚Äî hire confidently
They communicate daily. The most detailed 5-star reviews describe painters who gave updates every single day ‚Äî not just at start and end. Ask upfront how they'll communicate.
They show up when they say. Punctuality is mentioned in virtually every top painter review. "Arrived on time" in early reviews is the strongest predictor of a good outcome.
They explain each step clearly. Great painters narrate the job as they go ‚Äî what they're doing today, what comes next, why. This prevents surprise disputes at the end.
They clean up completely. "Left the yard spotless," "cleaned up after themselves," "moved furniture back" ‚Äî cleanup is specifically called out in nearly every 5-star painter review.
Change orders are handled easily. The best painters have a clear process for scope changes ‚Äî written, agreed on, priced before work continues.
! Red flags ‚Äî proceed carefully
No written scope before starting. Scope creep appears in 8.5% of all painter reviews. Without a written, room-by-room breakdown, "painting the house" means different things to different people.
They go quiet mid-project. The pattern in 1-star reviews: everything fine at start, then radio silence, then the job is "done" but half of what was agreed isn't finished.
Aggressive response to negative feedback. Multiple reviews describe painters who sent threatening messages or filed fake reports after receiving honest reviews. A combative online presence is a real signal.
Vague or verbal quotes only. "They said it would cost around X" is how almost every scope-creep story starts. If they won't put the itemized quote in writing, that tells you something.
No existing regulars or repeat customers. Painters have a relatively low return rate (3.1%) ‚Äî but the ones people do return to or recommend tend to be dramatically better than the average hire.
Questions to ask before you hire
Based on what actually generates 1-star reviews
1
Can you give me an itemized quote ‚Äî by room, surface, and prep work?
Scope creep is the #1 painter complaint. A quote that lists "full interior" is much easier to dispute than one that says "living room ceiling, 2 coats, including sanding and patching." The more specific, the safer you are.
2
How will you communicate with me during the project ‚Äî and how often?
42.6% of painter reviews mention communication. The ones that go badly almost always describe going silent. A great painter will have a clear answer. A bad one will be vague.
3
What is your process if the scope changes mid-project?
The best painters handle change orders in writing ‚Äî agreed and priced before any additional work starts. "We'll figure it out" is not an acceptable answer.
4
What prep work is included, and what does cleanup look like when you're done?
Cleanup is specifically called out in 5-star reviews more than almost anything else. Ask explicitly: do they move and replace furniture? Protect floors? Remove tape and drop cloths? Get this in writing.
5
Do you have references from jobs completed in the last 6 months?
Recent references matter more than old ones. A painter who did great work 2 years ago may have different crew now. Specifically ask for references on similar-sized jobs to yours.
6
What paint brand and finish do you recommend, and why?
Good painters have opinions about materials and can explain them. A vague answer ("whatever you want") can signal they're cutting corners on product quality.
What a good quote includes
19% of painter reviews mention the quote process ‚Äî here is what the good ones look like
Line items by room or surface ‚Äî not just a total number. "Living room: 2 coats + ceiling" vs. "interior painting."
Prep work explicitly listed ‚Äî sanding, patching holes, priming, caulking. These are often where costs balloon if not specified upfront.
Paint brand, product line, and sheen level ‚Äî "premium eggshell" vs. "standard flat" is a significant cost and quality difference.
Number of coats ‚Äî one coat is not the same as two. This should be explicit, especially for color changes or dark-to-light transitions.
Timeline and daily schedule ‚Äî what days, what hours, whether they work around your schedule.
Cleanup and furniture handling ‚Äî what they will and will not move, how they protect floors and fixtures.
Change order process ‚Äî how additional work gets priced and approved in writing before starting.
Payment milestones ‚Äî a reasonable painter asks for a deposit (not 100% upfront) with remaining balance on completion.

The bottom line
üñåÔ∏è
Painters are a relatively safe hire ‚Äî if you nail the quote
At 86.3% 5-star, painters outperform almost every other service category. The failure mode is almost always the same: a vague quote that becomes disputed scope, combined with a contractor who stops communicating. If you get an itemized written quote and ask one question ‚Äî "how will you communicate with me daily?" ‚Äî you've eliminated the two main risk factors.

The painters who consistently get named in 5-star reviews are ones where a specific person on the crew made an impression. When you're reading reviews, look for names. "Ask for Marcus" or "the crew led by Raul" is a stronger signal than a generic 5-star.
