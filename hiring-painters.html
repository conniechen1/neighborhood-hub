What separates good from bad
‚úì Hire confidently if...
They communicate daily. 43% of all painter reviews mention communication ‚Äî it's the strongest predictor of outcome.
They show up on time. Punctuality appears in nearly every 5-star review. Lateness is a canary.
They explain as they go. "Crew explained each step clearly" ‚Äî prevents end-of-job disputes.
Cleanup is explicit. "Left everything spotless" is one of the most common 5-star phrases. Ask what cleanup includes.
Change orders are written. Best painters price and approve scope changes before continuing.
! Watch out if...
The quote isn't itemized. "Full house paint" is not a quote. Scope creep hits 8.5% of jobs ‚Äî mostly vague original scopes.
They go quiet mid-project. The 1-star pattern: great start, then silence, then the job is "done" but half the scope wasn't finished.
They get hostile about reviews. Two painters in this dataset sent threatening messages to reviewers. A combative owner response online is a real red flag.
Materials aren't specified. "$1,718 for Dynasty Paint" ‚Äî inflated material costs are a common padding tactic. Ask for brand, product line, and quantity.
100% payment upfront. Legitimate painters ask for a deposit with balance on completion. Full upfront = no leverage if work is incomplete.
5 questions to ask before you hire
1
Can I get an itemized quote ‚Äî by room, surface, and prep work?
Scope creep is the #1 painter complaint. "Living room ceiling, 2 coats, sanding + patching" is a quote. "Interior painting" is not.
2
How will you communicate during the job, and how often?
43% of reviews mention communication. A great painter answers this confidently. Vagueness here predicts vagueness everywhere.
3
What brand and finish do you recommend for this surface, and why?
Good painters have opinions about materials. "Whatever you want" can mean they are planning to use the cheapest option available.
4
What does cleanup include ‚Äî furniture, floors, tape removal?
Cleanup is the most-mentioned detail in 5-star reviews. Get it in writing so there is no ambiguity at the end of the job.
5
What is your process for scope changes mid-project?
"We will figure it out" is not acceptable. The answer you want: written change order, agreed and priced before any additional work starts.
